Professional Summary

Plant scientist with a PhD in plant biotechnology, specializing in crop physiology, plant metabolism, trait characterization under complex abiotic stresses, multi-omics integration, and predictive modeling for climate-resilient crop performance.

Experience

  • 2025 - Present

    Be'er Sheva, Israel

    Postdoctoral Researcher in Plant Metabolomics and Physiology
    Ben-Gurion University of the Negev
    • Lead an OIV-funded integrative phenotyping project combining UAV-based thermal imaging, physiology, metabolomics, gene expression, and AI-assisted predictive modeling to identify climate-resilient varietal performance.
    • Design above- and below-ground crop physiology experiments using gas exchange, chlorophyll fluorescence, ionomics, plant biochemistry, and multi-year field and controlled-environment phenotyping.
    • Generate and analyze metabolomics and volatilomics datasets using GC-MS, LC-MS, HS-SPME-GC-MS, multivariate statistics, network analysis, and machine learning.
    • Integrate multi-omics datasets to resolve carbon metabolism, ion partitioning, root-zone processes, and biochemical regulation underlying abiotic stress responses.
  • 2021 - Present

    Addis Ababa, Ethiopia

    Researcher II, Plant Molecular Breeder
    Bio and Emerging Technology Institute
    • Led crop improvement research for dryland production systems, integrating physiological phenotyping, quantitative genetics, molecular tools, and stress-resilient trait validation.
    • Applied genomic-assisted selection and molecular breeding approaches to accelerate selection and deployment of improved crop varieties.
    • Coordinated multi-institutional, internationally funded research programs including BIOINNOVATE Africa.
  • 2014 - 2021

    Addis Ababa, Ethiopia

    Junior Researcher to Associate Researcher, Plant Breeder
    Ethiopian Institute of Agricultural Research
    • Designed and implemented breeding schemes, crossing, hybridization, and heterosis exploitation that led to three improved sorghum varieties and contributed to eight more released varieties as part of a team.
    • Led large-scale phenotyping and physiological characterization of sorghum germplasm across multi-environment trials.
    • Integrated field phenotyping, SNP-based genotyping, genotype-phenotype association analysis, and data-driven selection pipelines.
  • 2012 - 2014

    Amhara Sayint, Ethiopia

    Crop Production and Protection Expert, System Agronomist
    Amhara Bureau of Agriculture, Amhara Sayint Agricultural Office
    • Provided field-level agronomic diagnostics and advisory support for maize, wheat, barley, and haricot bean production.
    • Conducted seed quality assessment and certification for haricot bean production systems targeting local and export markets.
